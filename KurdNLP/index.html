Kurdish is an Indo-European language spoken by 20 million speakers, inhabitants of Kurdish regions of Turkey, Iran, Iraq and, Syria. Despite the considerable number of speakers who also use Kurdish on their electronic devices in their everyday lives, Kurdish is rarely technically supported online — from a simple spell checker plug-in to Kurdish interfaces— due to the lack of language processing tools.

KurdNLP project

We are aiming at creating a library for Kurdish language processing. We believe that today is the best time ever to fulfil such a project, particularly thanks to a plethora of Kurdish online resources, the vast accessibility of Kurdish speakers to the Internet which makes annotations related tasks feasible, and the recent techniques of machine learning in NLP which help us to overcome some of the challenges in processing low-resourced languages.

Project goals

In this phase of the project, we are focusing on the following tasks:

 Resources  Processing tools
 POS tagging corpus Text normaliser including sentence splitter, tokeniser, stemmer and lemmatiser 
 Morphological analyser
 Syntactical analyser
 Chunker

 

Kurdish is generally written in two orthographies: one based on the Latin orthography which is used by the Kurmanji speakers, mostly in Kurdistan of Turkey, and the other is based on the Arabic orthography which is used in the other regions of Kurdistan. This diversity in writing, not only adds to the gap between the dialects but also becomes a challenge in text processing. KurdNLP will provide tools for transliterating different orthographies and disambiguating challenging cases.

The POS tagging corpus is one of the most important parts of the project. We are currently looking for annotators to manually annotate our corpus which is based on the Pewan corpus. Once the corpus tagged, we will be able to provide morphological and syntactical analysers.

Project outcome

The outcome of the project will be presented as an open-source library in Python. In addition to this library, a dedicated website will provide the tools online. We will try to present our works as research papers as well.
