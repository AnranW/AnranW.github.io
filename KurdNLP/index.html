We are aiming at creating a basic language processing toolkit for Kurdish. We believe that today is the best time ever to fulfil such a project, particularly thanks to a plethora of Kurdish online resources, the vast accessibility of Kurdish speakers to the Internet--which makes annotation related tasks feasible, and the recent progresses thanks to machine learning in NLP-- which help to overcome some of the challenges in low-resourced languages processing.

Project goals

In this phase of the project, we are focusing on the following tasks:

Transliteration

Kurdish is generally written in two orthographies: one based on the Latin orthography which is used by the Kurmanji speakers, mostly in Kurdistan of Turkey, and the other is based on the Arabic orthography which is used in the other regions of Kurdistan. This diversity in writing, not only adds to the gap between the dialects but also becomes a challenge in text processing. Kurdish-BLARK will provide tools for transliterating different orthographies and disambiguating challenging cases.

PoS Tagging corpus

The POS tagging corpus is one of the most important parts of the project. We are currently looking for annotators to manually annotate our corpus which is based on the Pewan corpus. Once the corpus tagged, we will be able to provide morphological and syntactical analysers.

Project outcome

The outcome of the project will be presented as an open-source library in Python. In addition to this library, a dedicated website will provide the tools online. Our priority is to contribute to not only the scientific aspect of the research project by publishing the results, but also to the engineering aspect for public usage.
